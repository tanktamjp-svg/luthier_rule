Luthier

The Art of the Instrument - Rules Reference

Game Overview

[cite_start]

Players act as families of artisans satisfying patrons' requests for fine instruments, performances, and repairs[cite: 1849]. [cite_start]The goal is to earn the most Prestige Points over 6 rounds[cite: 2135, 2140].

    [cite_start]
  • Prestige: Earned by making instruments, satisfying patrons, performing, repairing, and claiming awards[cite: 2136].
  • Patron Patience: Patrons provide gifts as long as patience endures. [cite_start]If patience runs out, they abandon you, costing prestige[cite: 1851].

Setup Highlights

    [cite_start]
  • Start Player: Determined by the lowest number on the Luthier Family Tile[cite: 2085].
    • [cite_start]
  • Hand Limit: There is no explicit hand limit for cards, but storage has a limit of 9 materials of any type[cite: 2125].
    • [cite_start]
  • Starting Resources: Varies by Luthier Family Tile selected[cite: 2077].
    • [cite_start]
  • 2-Player Setup: Requires special Orchestra setup cards and cover-up tiles[cite: 1975, 1976].

Round Structure

The game is played over 6 rounds. Each round consists of:

  1. Start of Round:
      [cite_start]
    • Advance Patron patience cubes and gain gifts[cite: 2216].
      • [cite_start]
    • Place up to 2 instrument cards from hand to Roughing Bench[cite: 2217].
    2. [cite_start]
  2. Planning Phase: Place workers on locations[cite: 2239].
    3. [cite_start]
  3. Resolution Phase: Activate locations in order[cite: 2266].
    4. [cite_start]
  4. End of Round: Update turn order, refresh market/cards, advance round tracker[cite: 2335].

Planning & Worker Placement

[cite_start]

Placement: In turn order, place exactly one worker chip (face down) on a location[cite: 2240].

    [cite_start]
  • Apprentices: You may stack up to 3 apprentices on a worker to increase its skill by +1 per apprentice[cite: 2245, 2260].
  • Stacking: You may place on top of existing chips. [cite_start]Multiple chips from the same player are allowed[cite: 2242, 2243].
  • Worker Numbers: Workers #1, #3, #5 available at start. [cite_start]#2 unlocks Round 3, #4 unlocks Round 5[cite: 2252, 2350].

Resolution & Priority Order

Locations are activated one by one. When a location activates, reveal all chips. Actions are taken in Priority Order:

    [cite_start]
  1. Highest Skill: Worker Number + Apprentices[cite: 2291].
    2. [cite_start]
  2. Tie-Breaker: First placed goes first[cite: 2294].
[cite_start]Alternate Action: At any location (Main Board or Workbench), a player may always choose to take $2 instead of the location's action[cite: 2314].

The Market

This is a "free action" taken once per Resolution Phase on your turn. [cite_start]It does not require a worker[cite: 2277, 2279].

  • Buy/Sell: Animal, Wood, Metal. [cite_start]Prices fluctuate based on the round card[cite: 2318]. [cite_start]You cannot buy and sell the same material in one round[cite: 2321].
    • [cite_start]
  • Hire Apprentice: Cost is $4[cite: 2326].
  • Training: Advance Performance or Craft track. [cite_start]Cost is $15 per step[cite: 2326].
    • [cite_start]
  • Inspiration Discount: Spend 1 Inspiration to reduce the cost of an item/action by $1 (1:1 ratio)[cite: 2328].

Main Board Locations

1. [cite_start]Salon (Patrons) [cite: 2403]

  • Main Action: Take a face-up Patron card and place it on your board (max 3 active). [cite_start]Gain placement bonus immediately[cite: 2405].
    • [cite_start]
  • Alternate: Take 2 Inspiration and 1 Apprentice[cite: 2407].
    • [cite_start]
  • 4+ Skill Bonus: Move one patience cube left one step and gain the gift[cite: 2408].

2. [cite_start]Guild (Instruments) [cite: 2466]

    [cite_start]
  • Main Action: Take a face-up Instrument card into hand[cite: 2469].
    • [cite_start]
  • Alternate: Take 1 Metal[cite: 2471].
    • [cite_start]
  • 4+ Skill Bonus: Take 1 Material of choice AND place 1 instrument from hand onto Roughing Bench[cite: 2472].

3. [cite_start]Perform [cite: 2545]

  • Main Action: Choose performance card. Roll dice + Worker Skill. Gain money/prestige. [cite_start]If Medium/High success, place token in Orchestra[cite: 2527, 2530].
    • [cite_start]
  • Alternate: Take 3 Animal[cite: 2533].
    • [cite_start]
  • 4+ Skill Bonus: Advance on Performance Skill Track[cite: 2534].
    • [cite_start]
  • Inspiration: Spend after rolling to increase result (+1 per token)[cite: 2540].

4. [cite_start]Repair [cite: 2589]

  • Main Action: Pay materials listed on Repair card. Gain prestige. [cite_start]Place repair token in Orchestra[cite: 2594].
    • [cite_start]
  • Alternate: Take 2 Wood[cite: 2604].
    • [cite_start]
  • 4+ Skill Bonus: Advance on Craft Skill Track[cite: 2605].
    • [cite_start]
  • Inspiration: 2 Inspiration = 1 Material substitute[cite: 2607].

5. [cite_start]Balcony [cite: 2624]

    [cite_start]
  • Main Action: Score an achieved Public Award[cite: 2632].
    • [cite_start]
  • Alternate: Take $6 OR Take 2 Apprentices[cite: 2633, 2634].
    • [cite_start]
  • 4+ Skill Bonus: Advance on Reputation Track[cite: 2635].
    • [cite_start]
  • Turn Order: Workers here determine next round's turn order[cite: 2638].

  • 3+ Skill Bonus: Gain 2 Inspiration[cite: 2735].

The Orchestra & First Chair

Tokens are placed here via Performing, Repairing, or Finishing Instruments.

[cite_start]

First Chair Rules [cite: 2830]

The "First Chair" is the top slot of a section. [cite_start]Only one token can occupy it (except for Rare Instruments)[cite: 2835, 2757].

    [cite_start]
  • Empty Seat: Place token, claim bonus[cite: 2835].
  • Instrument Token: Always steals the chair from Performance/Repair tokens. [cite_start]Moves previous token "below the line"[cite: 2838].
    • [cite_start]
  • Performance/Repair Token: Can only steal if you now have more tokens in that section than the current owner[cite: 2846].
    • [cite_start]
  • Below the Line: If you cannot claim First Chair, place token below the line and gain $2 OR 1 Inspiration[cite: 2844].

End Game Scoring

The game ends after Round 6. Add the following to current Prestige:

    [cite_start]
  1. First Chairs: Score points based on total chairs held (e.g., 1 chair = 4pts, 6 chairs = 139pts)[cite: 2890, 2905].
    2. [cite_start]
  2. Patron (B) Powers: Score variable points based on card text[cite: 2896].
    3. [cite_start]
  3. Unfinished Instruments: Instruments on Finishing Bench score half prestige (rounded up)[cite: 2896].
    4. [cite_start]
  4. Personal Goals: Score highest level achieved[cite: 2902].
    5. [cite_start]
  5. Specialty Workers: 1 Point per card[cite: 2903].
  6. Leftovers: 1 Point per 10 total value (money + materials + inspiration + apprentices). [cite_start]Use money as tie-breaker[cite: 2904, 2908].
